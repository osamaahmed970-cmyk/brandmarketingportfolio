01 — Product Origin

I Didn't Just Run This Campaign. I Started It.

"The client wanted a sweet and spicy crispy chicken burger. I suggested we add a beef patty — and combine both into one."

During the product shoot, Osama tried the original crispy chicken concept and proposed a beef patty variant on the spot. When both were tested together, the idea evolved further: combine the crispy chicken and beef patty into a single dual-protein burger — a format that had never been done in Canada — with Tabasco's sweet and spicy sauce as the flavor anchor.

Building on the success of the earlier BIG 3 campaign (which doubled existing burgers and resonated strongly with customers), this concept carried the same energy — bold, craveable, and unique to Boardwalk.

Product Concept
Canada's first sweet and spicy beef + chicken duo burger.

Concept originated on-site during a product shoot. Pitched, approved, and launched as the hero product of the Sweet Heat campaign.
02 — Brand Collaboration

Tabasco × Coca-Cola × Boardwalk Burgers

Sweet Heat was a three-way co-brand partnership. Managing this meant more than just running ads — it required aligning creative assets, copy, and visual identity across three distinct brand guidelines simultaneously, while also negotiating and managing co-marketing funding from both Tabasco and Coca-Cola to support the campaign.

06 — Key Learnings

What This Campaign Proved

✅
Product Thinking is a Marketing Skill
The most impactful contribution to this campaign happened before a single ad was written — in the product shoot. Marketers who understand the product deeply enough to improve it create differentiation that no ad budget can buy.
✅
Co-Brand Events Create Earned Media
The influencer launch event generated authentic UGC across 20+ creators simultaneously — content that would have cost significantly more to produce through traditional production. Events as media strategy.
💡
Co-Funding Extends Campaign Reach
Securing marketing dollars from brand partners means the campaign spend effectively goes further. Building these co-brand relationships requires compliance management overhead but delivers real budget leverage.
💡
Brand Compliance at Scale
Managing creative approvals across three distinct brand standards simultaneously requires systematic workflows. Building approval templates and clear brand checkpoints from the start prevents revision cycles.
